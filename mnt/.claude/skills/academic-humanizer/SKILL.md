# Academic Text Humanizer

AI generated academic text fails because it optimizes for completeness at the expense of authentic intellectual struggle. Transform output into prose reflecting how trained researchers actually communicate.

## Core Principle

Academic writing is social action. Papers stake claims, manage relationships, and position writers within communities. Surface features emerge from these functions. Replicate the functions, not just the features.

## Revision Sequence

Apply transformations from deep structure to surface form.

### 1. Information Structure

Begin sentences with given information, end with new. Implement reference chains degrading from full nominal to pronoun as entities become established.

Wrong: A significant relationship was found between X and Y. This finding contradicts previous research.

Right: Previous research suggested no relationship between X and Y. The present findings contradict this view, revealing significant association.

### 2. Hedging Architecture

Stratify by claim type. Direct findings: light hedging (the results indicate). Interpretations: moderate (this may suggest). Speculative extensions: strong (future research might explore). Add strategic boosting at emphasis points.

Wrong: The results may possibly suggest that there might be an association.

Right: The results indicate significant association. This finding suggests theoretical frameworks emphasizing isolated factors may require revision.

### 3. Sentence Rhythm

Vary length deliberately: medium declarative, longer elaboration, short conclusion. Target 8 to 35 words per sentence. Include one very short and one notably long paragraph per section.

### 4. Register Modulation

Introduction/Discussion: broader vocabulary, complex syntax, higher abstraction
Methods: simpler syntax, technical vocabulary, procedural concreteness
Results: balance precision with accessibility

### 5. Clause Combining

Incorporate non finite participial clauses, appositives, reduced relatives, multiple embedding levels. AI relies on simple coordination.

Simple: We used topic modeling. This helped identify themes.

Complex: Using topic modeling to analyse the corpus, we identified themes relating to generational differences, a pattern consistent with prior computational approaches.

### 6. Metadiscourse

Remove functionless markers. Add guidance at genuine transitions. Deploy hedges, boosters, attitude markers strategically rather than uniformly.

### 7. Stance and Evaluation

Add attitude markers where findings diverge from expectations. Express genuine surprise with phrases like contrary to expectations and interestingly when warranted.

### 8. Concession

Incorporate substantive concession with although, while, despite constructions. Anticipate objections before readers formulate them.

### 9. Disciplinary Positioning

Establish theoretical tradition through foundational citations, appropriate terminology, demonstrated understanding of debates.

Wrong: There is a gap in the literature.

Right: Existing research has begun to unravel generational differences but shies away from comprehensive examination of workplace technology adoption.

### 10. Productive Imperfection

Retain minor imperfections signaling human production. Allow slight repetition. Permit constructions prioritizing meaning over elegance.

## Section Guidance

Introduction: Ground openings in specifics. Use temporal markers, concessive phrases, geographic grounding. Position within scholarly conversations.

Literature Review: Synthesize multiple sources into integrated claims. Maintain lexical cohesion through vocabulary chaining around core concepts.

Methods: Past tense for procedures. Include specific tool names, version numbers, parameters.

Results: Past tense for findings, present for interpretive comments.

Discussion: Broader register. Express intellectual uncertainty. Connect to theory. Acknowledge limitations substantively.

## Avoid

Three item parallel lists where one word suffices. Unnecessary intensifiers (very, highly, extremely). Uniform paragraph lengths. Mechanical citation placement at sentence ends. Overexplaining common concepts while underexplaining novel ones.
