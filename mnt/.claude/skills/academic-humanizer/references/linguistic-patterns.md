# Linguistic Patterns for Academic Humanization

Comprehensive patterns extracted from peer reviewed sources across computational, qualitative, economic, and psychological methodologies.

## Thematic Progression

Linear progression: new information of sentence one becomes theme of sentence two.

Example: Dating app feedback can be considered a way of deepening self perception. As mentioned the number of matches one makes on dating apps is a form of dating app feedback.

Constant progression: multiple sentences share the same theme with varied new information.

Map progression across paragraphs. Restructure sentences violating given to new ordering by moving established information to subject position.

## Lexical Cohesion

Maintain conceptual vocabulary chains throughout documents. Core concepts should appear in varied surface forms while maintaining referential continuity.

Example chain: generation, generational, intergenerational, age groups, older workers, younger employees, Baby Boomers, Millennials, Generation Z

AI often introduces synonyms inconsistently or loses track of key terminology. Identify core vocabulary and maintain systematic chaining.

## Reference Chains

Track entities across text through pronouns, demonstratives, and definite noun phrases following natural degradation.

Pattern: full nominal at first mention, then the + noun, then shortened form, then pronoun

Example: Istria County (first mention) → the county → the region → Istria → it

AI either maintains full nominal reference unnaturally or loses track of referents. Implement degradation while ensuring retrievability when distance from antecedent increases.

## Lexical Bundles

Common academic bundles by function:

Referential: the nature of, the relationship between, the extent to which, in terms of
Discourse organizing: in the present study, as shown in, in the following section
Stance: it is possible that, it should be noted, the fact that

Deploy with variable frequency, clustering in some passages while absent from others. AI spaces bundles evenly.

## Nominalization

Transform verbal concepts into nominals for theoretical discussion: adopt → adoption, implement → implementation, integrate → integration

Functions: enables thematic progression, creates appropriate abstraction, achieves economy

Balance: nominalize in theoretical passages, preserve verbal constructions in procedural descriptions. Excessive nominalization produces impenetrable prose.

## Temporal Reference

Present tense: general truths, established theoretical claims (social comparison theory posits)
Past tense: specific study findings, completed procedures (research indicated, participants were instructed)
Present perfect: ongoing research traditions (scholars have examined)

Methods: past tense throughout
Results: past for findings, present for interpretive comments
Discussion: present for implications, past for study specific observations

## Citation Integration

Integral citations: name authors in sentence grammar for thematic prominence
Non integral citations: append parenthetically to focus on propositional content

Vary form based on whether authorial attribution or content deserves emphasis. AI places citations mechanically at sentence ends.

Citation verbs by relationship:
Agreement: confirms, supports, aligns with, is consistent with
Extension: builds on, expands, advances
Contrast: however, in contrast to, unlike, challenges

Synthesize multiple sources into integrated claims rather than presenting sequentially.

## Clause Types

Coordinate clauses: parallel findings in results sections
Subordinate clauses: logical relationships in introduction/discussion
Relative clauses: characterizing cited works in literature reviews
Non finite participial: efficient modification in methods

Example: Using topic modeling we analyse large data sets and uncover patterns and themes in generational interactions with digital technologies at work

Combines non finite participial clause, coordinate verb phrases, and prepositional phrase with complex nominal modification.

## Metadiscourse Categories

Interactive (organize discourse):
Transition markers: however, therefore, in addition (at paragraph boundaries)
Frame markers: turning now to, in terms of (at section openings)
Endophoric markers: as noted above, see Table 1 (increase as paper progresses)
Evidentials: according to, as demonstrated by
Code glosses: in other words, that is, for example (more frequent in theory sections)

Interactional (involve readers):
Hedges: may, might, could, appear to, suggest (one per two to three sentences in interpretation)
Boosters: clearly, certainly, definitely (sparingly, for well established claims)
Attitude markers: importantly, interestingly, surprisingly (at genuine evaluative moments)
Self mention: we argue, our findings (for positioning, disappear during procedures)
Engagement markers: it is not surprising that (presuppose shared frameworks)

## Paragraph Structure

Hourglass pattern: general opening → narrow evidence/examples → general implications

AI produces flat structure with uniform generality. Vary abstraction levels across paragraph positions.

Vary paragraph length: some two sentences, others eight or nine. AI defaults to four to five sentences uniformly.

## Handling Contradiction

Acknowledge contradictory findings explicitly. Explain discrepancies through moderating factors or methodological differences.

Example: Some studies emphasise significant cultural differences while others find minimal variation. This pattern suggests contextual moderators shape observed relationships.

Report null findings with theoretical explanation rather than ignoring them.

Example: Gender did not moderate the mediation path contrary to expectations. This null finding may reflect changing social norms around body image across genders.

## Concession Markers

Initial position concessive + main clause asserting position:
Although the concept is difficult to conceptualize, the present framework offers operational clarity.
Despite methodological limitations, the findings extend theoretical understanding.
While one might argue alternative explanations exist, the evidence more strongly supports the proposed mechanism.

Anticipatory concession: raise and address objections before readers formulate them.

## Stance Gradation

Strong claim (boosted): The results clearly demonstrate
Moderate claim (light hedge): The results indicate
Interpretive claim (moderate hedge): This finding suggests
Speculative claim (strong hedge): One possible explanation might be
Future direction (strongest hedge): Future research could potentially explore

Match stance strength to evidence quality and claim type.

## Managing Known vs New

Introduce new entities with indefinite reference (a study, one factor)
Refer subsequently with definite reference (the study, this factor)

Presupposition triggers signal established information: factive verbs (know, realize), cleft constructions, definite descriptions

Calibrate to target reader knowledge. Do not define regression for quantitative journal. Do define novel theoretical constructs.

## Voice Distribution

Passive voice: methodological procedures for replicability (data was gathered, participants were instructed), when agent genuinely unknown or unimportant

Active voice: interpretive claims with authorial responsibility (we argue, our findings suggest)

Strategic distribution creates appropriate responsibility attribution.

## Authenticity Markers

Allow minor inconsistencies: varying spellings, repeated transitional phrases, constructions prioritizing meaning over elegance

Express genuine intellectual puzzlement: contrary to expectations, surprisingly, it remains unclear why

Position within ongoing debates: characterize camps, unresolved questions, methodological disputes

AI rarely expresses surprise because it lacks expectations to violate. Include authentic evaluative moments.
